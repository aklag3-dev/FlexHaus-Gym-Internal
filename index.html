§1 — Tara • Diagnosis

The Second-Class Cliff

Data source: flexhaus_bookings.csv — 45,349 booking rows, 1,556 distinct members, 18 months (Dec 2024 – May 2026), full export (not a sample). Member panel rebuilt via GROUP BY member_id. The five required analyses below.

Analysis 1: Member Panel Reconstruction

1,556 total members identified: 637 active, 886 cancelled, 33 frozen. 637 active members generate ~€30,800/month in recurring revenue at an average €45.26/mo.

Analysis 3: The At-Risk Segment

Defined as: active (paying) members who show disengagement — either only 1 booking ever (one-and-done) or no visit in 30+ days (lapsed multi-bookers). These members have not cancelled yet, so a single good nudge can still land.

Analysis 4: Easiest Re-Entry Point

Among members who did book a second class, Spin (174), HIIT (163), and Yoga (156) were the most common entry points. Weekday afternoons (Thu/Fri) and Sunday showed highest second-class conversion. No-show rates are consistent across class types (~7–8%), so no single class is a barrier.

Analysis 5: What Predicts a Second Class

The top re-engagement lever is familiarity + low friction. Members who came via friend referral have the highest second-class rate. The most effective nudge combines: the member's first class type (they already chose it once) + an off-peak slot with availability + a warm personal line ("we saved you a spot").

Three Root Causes (Each Data-Backed)

  • No structured onboarding past Class 1: 42% of members (655) never book a second class. There is no automatic follow-up, no "you might like this" recommendation, no incentive to return. The system treats a trial as success.
  • Zero re-engagement for the paying-but-absent: Active members who haven't visited in 30+ days (96 members, €4,318/mo) receive no check-in. The gym collects their money and stays silent — until they cancel.
  • No personalisation in the booking experience: Members don't see their favourite class or a pre-selected slot. The generic timetable is a wall of choices, which the disengaged member bounces from.

The One Metric This Campaign Exists to Move

Comeback-class bookings. A booking from an at-risk member is the single north-star metric. Everything — the three.js page, the win-back sequence, the social content — serves to drive one action: a disengaged member booking their next class. Target: 50 comeback bookings in 4 weeks from the 254 at-risk members.

§2 — Mateo • Design Spec

"The Comeback" — WebGL Booking Experience

A scroll-driven three.js journey: a resting heartbeat pulse that accelerates as the member scrolls through a stylised gym space, ending in a one-tap booking card bathed in Volt-green light.

① Scene 1: The Resting Pulse

Slow heartbeat line of light in dark space. Headline appears: "Your spot's still here." Sub: one-line no-shame promise. CTA: Book your comeback class.

② Momentum Builds

Camera fly-through of stylised gym (rig, studio) with chalk-dust GPU particles. Pulse accelerates. 2–3 lines reframing the lapse as a return, not a failure.

③ The Proof

Three "why now" panels rise: one class to restart, your favourite on Tuesday, we held the spot. One honest stat: "75% of one-timers never come back — you're still here."

④ The Comeback Card

Volt-green pool of light. Booking card with class + slot pre-selected. One-tap form. Success: the card pulses, the heartbeat line races to full.

Technical Specifications

Rendering: three.js r160 via CDN import map. GPU Points for chalk-dust particle system. Three.js Line for heartbeat pulse. Postprocessing bloom for Volt-green glow.

Scroll choreography: Camera position + pulse speed lerped by scroll progress. Smooth damp, no jank. Mobile-first: touch-scroll friendly.

Performance: Interactive < 2.5s on mid-range phone. Target 60fps, floor 30fps on mobile.

Light mode palette: Chalk #F5F5F2 background (light field), Carbon #0E1116 text, Volt #C6F24E accents, Ember #FF5A3C sparingly for warmth.

Fallbacks: prefers-reduced-motion serves a static hero. No-WebGL detection renders a styled hero + full booking form. Both ensure the booking flow works with zero 3D.

A11y: Canvas aria-hidden="true". All content in DOM. Keyboard-reachable form, visible focus rings, real labels.
